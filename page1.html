Won't I be isolated?

You may be worried that if you go tiny it means giving up family dinners, entertaining friends, or having parties. Entertaining in a tiny home is different, but in a wonderful way. We’ve put together tips to get you started.

Take the party outside.

There’s nothing like a fall day surrounded by friends and family outside. Play games like corn hole or frisbree, start a campfire, or even grill.

Entertaining outdoors brings a whole new layer of fun and possibilities for activities.

Skip the Buffet.

Instead of a buffet, serve guests with plated food. It will save everyone from trying to walk through the house and kitchen, and you’ll look fancier too.

Prep & cook early.

Prep &
cook early.

Prep and cook food for guests before they arrive so you can enjoy the beautiful outdoors and weather with them. No more yelling from the kitchen or struggling to follow the conversation while running a mixer.

Decorate the outdoor space.

If you’re the type that loves decorating for every holiday, then bring your decorating magic to the outdoor space. There are so many ways to be creative outside, and you’ll surely find ways to impress guests. It doesn’t even have to be for a holiday! A set of string lights and a few chairs can set the mood.
